# How To Last Longer In Bed - 100% REAL

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vucRWfo5csM

## TL;DR

A long, rambling vlog where the creator wraps sexual-performance advice inside a broader “animal lifestyle” routine: train, eat clean, hydrate, avoid smoking/vaping/alcohol/drugs, manage stress, and practice control. The useful core is not a magic supplement; it is arousal management, communication, consent, foreplay, stop-start pacing, pelvic-floor awareness, and prioritising the partner’s experience.

## Key points

- **Lifestyle baseline matters** — his frame is that general health affects performance: gym, legs/cardio/bodyweight training, clean food, protein/fruit, water, avoiding sugar/bad food, and avoiding vaping/smoking/alcohol/drugs. He admits people can still perform without perfect habits, but says this is the “best of the best” setup.

- **Stress is the main enemy** — he repeatedly says stress is the biggest performance killer. Bad days happen; quick ejaculation or erection variability does not mean someone is broken or unattractive.

- **No magic pill** — he is skeptical of performers/influencers selling miracle products. His claim: improvement comes from practice, conditioning, and communication, not a secret supplement.

- **Train yourself not to rush** — if masturbating, he recommends making it deliberately long rather than quick, because the body/mind gets conditioned by the pattern. His number: **20 minutes minimum**, max **twice per day**, with stop-start when close.

- **Pre-emptive release can lower arousal** — if meeting someone later, he says masturbating **1.5–2 hours before** can reduce excess horniness and make control easier. Not a universal rule; depends on refractory period and age.

- **Familiar partner is easier than new partner** — with a loyal recurring partner, both people learn each other’s body and preferences, so performance stress drops. With a new partner, novelty and sensitivity make control harder.

- **Use foreplay as pacing, not filler** — kiss, touch, oral, hands, hugging, taking breaks. His key idea: you do not only have penetration; use everything else to keep the partner satisfied while your arousal comes down.

- **Stop-start during sex** — when you feel ejaculation “from far,” pause penetration, switch to kissing/hands/oral, breathe, communicate, then resume. He says the first **5–15 minutes** are often hardest; after that, control gets easier as you acclimate.

- **Communication is central** — ask if she is comfortable, what she likes, whether she needs anything, and say honestly if you are too aroused and need to slow down. He repeats “communication is the key.”

- **Consent is mandatory** — near the end he explicitly says you must ask before doing things, be respectful, and not force yourself or the other person into something unwanted.

- **Partner-first mindset** — his north star is “make her feel good” before worrying about your own finish. If you finish early, don’t stop: rest, drink water, use hands/mouth, take care of her, and possibly go again.

- **Pelvic-floor / PC muscle training** — he describes the muscle used to stop urination. Suggested drills: repeated contractions, e.g. **3 sets of 50**, plus long holds of **10–60 seconds**, while breathing. He says this does not give 100% control but helps you feel the point of no return earlier.

- **Normalize imperfection** — some days are 3–10 minutes, some are 30–90 minutes. He says it is not a competition; compatibility, mood, stress, and novelty matter.

## Practical checklist

- Build baseline: sleep enough, train, hydrate, eat decently, avoid vaping/smoking/alcohol/drugs where possible.
- Do not train yourself to rush every time; if solo, practice slow stop-start control.
- If very aroused before a date, consider pre-emptive release only if you know your body recovers well.
- Start slow: kissing, touching, oral/hands, comfort checks.
- When close: stop penetration early, breathe, switch activities, resume later.
- Communicate plainly; don’t turn performance into silent pressure.
- If you finish early, keep taking care of partner instead of panicking.
- Train pelvic-floor control, but do not expect magic.

## One-line summary

His advice boils down to: get healthier, reduce stress, practice slow arousal control, communicate, use foreplay/pauses intelligently, and make the partner’s experience the priority.
