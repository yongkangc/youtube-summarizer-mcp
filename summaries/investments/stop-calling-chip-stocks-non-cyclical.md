# Stop Calling These Chip Stocks Non-Cyclical

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cgnLt6nC74E

## TL;DR

The video argues that memory and AI semiconductor stocks are still cyclical even if the current AI-driven cycle lasts longer than usual. The right question is not “cyclical or non-cyclical?” but “how short, sensitive, and economically linked is the revenue cycle?” Memory may be in a longer-than-normal upcycle, but that does not change the underlying business model.

## Key Insights

- “Cyclical” needs a sharper definition.
  - A cyclical business has revenue and profits that move with the broader economy.
  - It over-earns during expansion and under-earns during slowdown.
  - But cycles can be short, long, or so long/stable that they look non-cyclical.

- Short-cycle businesses are more sensitive to GDP and spending changes.
  - If GDP growth accelerates, short-cycle companies often see revenue quickly rise.
  - If GDP growth slows, even without an official recession, revenue can slow or fall.
  - The speaker argues 2022–2023 felt recessionary for many businesses despite official GDP not showing a classic recession because inflation distorted nominal spending.

- Sectors are too crude for cycle classification.
  - Information technology includes both semiconductors and SaaS.
  - Semiconductors are closer to manufacturing/industrials and usually cyclical.
  - SaaS subscription businesses often have much steadier revenue.
  - Communication services includes both stable mobile bills and cyclical advertising businesses like Google/Meta.

- Memory is still short-cycle / cyclical.
  - Micron is the main example.
  - The current AI/memory cycle may last several years, possibly through the decade.
  - But a long upcycle does not make memory non-cyclical.
  - The 2015–2018 Micron run was also a multi-year upcycle, yet the business remained cyclical.

- The key distinction is business economics, not stock price.
  - A cyclical business can have a great stock run.
  - A non-cyclical business can be bought at the wrong valuation and perform badly.
  - Stock-price behavior is not the same as business-cycle classification.

- Examples used:
  - **Micron**: short-cycle, sensitive, cyclical memory business.
  - **Intuitive Surgical**: hardware, but long-cycle; new da Vinci generations create multi-year growth waves.
  - **Vertex**: healthcare/pharma, generally non-cyclical, steady revenue growth.
  - **Adobe**: software/SaaS-like, relatively non-cyclical revenue profile.
  - **NextEra Energy**: utility, very stable/non-cyclical.
  - **Credo**: young AI interconnect/semi business, likely short-cycle but still early; strong momentum and expected **80% revenue growth** in fiscal 2027 with **50% adjusted profit margins**.
  - **Palo Alto Networks**: tricky; appears steady because of M&A, but underlying cybersecurity product cycles can be fast. Organic growth was cited at **14% YoY**, with AI accelerating bookings.

## Framework for Analyzing Revenue Cycles

Ask:

- Who are the primary customers?
- Is the business B2B or consumer?
- Is revenue concentrated?
- What exactly is being monetized: commodity, product, service, platform, or subscription?
- Is the offering differentiated or replaceable?
- Why do customers choose this company over alternatives?
- Why and how do customers spend money here?
- What are the risks: seasonality, churn, order timing, customer concentration, end-market weakness?

The speaker emphasizes going beyond superficial answers. For example, “Google sells ads” is not enough; you need to understand the software, auction, targeting, and customer ROI mechanism behind ad monetization.

## Investor Read-Through

The practical point: don’t let AI hype turn a cyclical hardware thesis into a fake “non-cyclical” thesis. If the business sells capacity-constrained hardware into a capex cycle, it can have a huge secular tailwind and still remain cyclical.

For memory specifically:

- AI/HBM can extend the cycle.
- Supply discipline and capex timing matter.
- Customer concentration and ordering behavior matter.
- The current cycle may be longer than usual, but downturn risk does not disappear.

## One-Line Summary

AI may lengthen the semiconductor and memory cycle, but it does not repeal cyclicality; investors need to understand revenue mechanics, customer behavior, and where the cycle can break.
