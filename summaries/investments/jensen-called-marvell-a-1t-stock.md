# Jensen Called Marvell a $1T Stock — We're Not Buying It

## TL;DR

Chip Stock Investor likes Marvell’s AI/custom silicon + networking exposure, but thinks the stock now prices in too much after the Jensen/Nvidia halo. At roughly **$245/share**, their reverse DCF implies about **47% per-share profit growth for five years**, using **50/50 EPS + FCF**, **10% discount rate**, and **5% terminal growth**. Their conclusion: good company, but valuation bar too high; they would pass and look for better semi values.

## Key points

- **Marvell has momentum** — stock ran hard after AI/custom ASIC excitement, recent acquisitions, and Jensen Huang calling Marvell a possible next **$1T** company.
- **They push back on the $1T framing** — their prior view: next $1T AI/semi names are more likely **AMD and/or Intel**, not Marvell.
- **Business model** — Marvell is a fabless chip designer and IP licensor, especially to hyperscalers building custom AI chips / ASICs / XPUs.
- **“Baby Broadcom” analogy** — Marvell benefits from custom silicon IP plus associated networking/connectivity demand, similar to Broadcom but smaller.
- **Revenue acceleration is real** — Q1 FY2027 revenue was just over **$2.4B**, up **28% YoY**. Q2 midpoint guide: **$2.7B**, up **35% YoY**.
- **Growth drivers**:
  - hyperscaler custom AI chip/IP licensing
  - networking systems for AI data centers
  - interconnect across GPUs, data-center parts, and data centers
  - Nvidia ecosystem partnership / NVLink-related role
- **Why Jensen talks it up** — Marvell helps some customers customize Nvidia-based data-center architectures. Nvidia also has a small equity stake. So Jensen praise is good ecosystem PR, not neutral valuation evidence.
- **CFO change viewed positively** — new CFO **Dan Durn** came from Adobe and previously Applied Materials / NXP. They like him and see him as useful for integrating acquisitions and managing next growth leg.
- **Acquisitions matter** — especially **Celestial AI**, tied to next-gen optical networking. They think acquisitions add growth but also create integration, FCF, debt, and dilution issues.
- **Profitability nuance** — Marvell has been FCF positive for years, but FCF is currently suppressed by acquisition integration and new product/customer ramps. GAAP profitability is now higher than FCF.
- **Balance sheet shifted** — Nvidia cash infusion pushed cash above **$2B**; long-term debt rose due to acquisitions; short-term debt appears refinanced into long-term debt.
- **Dilution increased** — shares rose from about **856M** at FY2026 end to **890M+** in Q1 FY2027, due to stock issuance for acquisitions. This offsets some per-share growth.
- **FY2028 outlook** — CEO expects data-center revenue to grow about **55% YoY** in FY2028, accelerating from FY2027. Total FY2028 annual revenue outlook: **$16.5B**, higher than prior guide.
- **Reverse DCF** — at about **$245/share**, fair value needs **47% average per-share profit growth** over the next five years.
- **Their issue** — much of near-term growth happens in FY2027/FY2028; harder question is what growth looks like in calendar **2028–2030** after current ramp.
- **Verdict** — they liked it when cheap in December and momentum-backed in March, but now think valuation is elevated and prefer other semiconductor opportunities.

## Investor read-through

- **Bull case:** Marvell has real AI infrastructure exposure: custom ASIC IP, networking, optical/interconnect, Nvidia ecosystem pull-through, and hyperscaler demand.
- **Bear case:** market may be paying peak AI-bottleneck multiple before proof that growth persists beyond current hyperscaler/custom silicon ramp.
- **Key risk:** stock issuance and acquisition integration dilute per-share economics. Revenue growth alone not enough; need EPS/FCF/share compounding.
- **Most important KPI:** FY2028 data-center growth and FCF conversion. If revenue accelerates but FCF/share lags due to integration/capex/dilution, valuation looks stretched.
- **YK read:** this is not “Marvell bad.” It is “Marvell already repriced.” Edge probably sits in finding next supplier with real design wins before Jensen/AI-bottleneck narrative fully infects valuation.

## One-line summary

Marvell is a real AI-infra beneficiary, but at **~$245** the stock seems to require **~47% per-share profit growth for five years**, so Chip Stock Investor says the Jensen $1T hype is ahead of the underwriting.
