# NVIDIA & Coherent: Reindustrializing America, Manufacturing for the AI Era

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GsqW5MPFajw

## TL;DR

Jensen Huang and Coherent CEO Jim Anderson frame Coherent’s Sherman, Texas indium phosphide expansion as a core AI-infrastructure bottleneck: AI factories need millions of processors connected across hundreds/thousands of feet, and that requires optical interconnects built from lasers/silicon photonics. Jensen’s bigger pitch: AI creates a new industrial stack — energy, chips, infrastructure, cloud, models, applications — and this is the right moment for the US to reindustrialize around advanced manufacturing rather than old commodity manufacturing.

## Core Points

- Coherent Sherman is positioned as AI connectivity infrastructure.
  - Site is described as the world’s first and largest-volume 6-inch indium phosphide manufacturing facility.
  - Coherent announced a $50M CHIPS award plus support from Texas/Sherman and Nvidia partnership investment.
  - Nvidia and Coherent have been partners/customer-supplier for over two decades.
  - Partnership aims to double facility size and quadruple output.

- Jensen’s AI stack is broader than models.
  - Today’s visible AI is the model/browser interface, but full stack includes:
    - energy sector
    - chips
    - infrastructure: land, power, shell
    - cloud services
    - AI models
    - applications
  - Models will not just be language models; also physical AI, robotics, biology/chemistry, drug discovery, physics, climate/weather simulation.
  - Applications touch financial services, healthcare, education, transportation, chip design, manufacturing, etc.

- AI is “ultimate general-purpose technology.”
  - Intelligence = processing information, reasoning, problem-solving.
  - Since every industry uses those, AI can transform every industry.
  - Jensen frames this as a new industrial revolution plus a new industry in itself.

- Connectivity becomes as important as compute.
  - AI factories require massive distributed computing: giant problems are sharded across hundreds of thousands or millions of processors.
  - Local links can use electrical interconnects.
  - Longer distances across racks/data centers require optical/silicon photonics.
  - Coherent’s indium phosphide lasers convert electrical signals to photons; optics carry signal; detectors convert photons back to electricity.
  - This is how processors separated by hundreds or thousands of feet act as one computer.

- Optical interconnect is a scaling bottleneck.
  - Jensen gives autonomous drug-discovery lab example: AI reads documents, reasons, uses lab robots/tools, receives measurements, then continues reasoning.
  - That loop needs extremely fast distributed compute.
  - As AI systems scale, interconnect/optical networking becomes central to performance, not secondary plumbing.

- US manufacturing reindustrialization is the political/economic frame.
  - Jensen says US chip fabs declined through his career, but are now growing again.
  - New buildout includes chip fabs, packaging plants, computer plants, manufacturing plants, data centers, and energy generation plants.
  - He claims AI-related investment has already created ~600,000 jobs in recent years.
  - Sherman site expected to support 550+ direct jobs and 1,000+ direct/indirect jobs at full capacity.

- Jensen’s argument: reindustrialize into new categories, not old ones.
  - US should not try to regain commodity manufacturing by competing against industries others have optimized for 50–100 years.
  - Better path: build manufacturing for new sectors that did not exist or were too small before.
  - AI creates demand for precisely those advanced plants: photonics, fabs, packaging, computers, power infrastructure.

- Energy is foundational.
  - Jensen repeatedly links pro-energy policy to fabs/plants/economic growth.
  - Without power, there are no fabs, data centers, or industrial growth.
  - He argues AI demand creates market-driven funding for solar, nuclear, and grid upgrades without needing traditional subsidies.

- Social/political pitch: bring back builders.
  - Jensen says economy cannot be only information workers/PhD computer scientists.
  - Manufacturing jobs allow people who build with their hands to have prosperous lives/families.
  - He links reindustrialization to community health, social harmony, national security, and balanced society.

## Investor Read-Through

- **Coherent / opticals:** video is direct validation that optical components are not peripheral to AI; they are part of the scale-out bottleneck. Indium phosphide lasers, detectors, transceivers, silicon photonics, and optical networking should be tracked as AI factory capex line items.

- **Nvidia:** Jensen is expanding AI-factory narrative beyond GPUs into full-system control: compute + networking + optical + power + manufacturing ecosystem. This supports the “Nvidia as systems company / AI infrastructure prime contractor” thesis.

- **US reindustrialization:** investment opportunity is not generic reshoring. It is specific advanced-manufacturing capacity tied to AI bottlenecks: leading-edge fabs, packaging, optics, power gear, data-center construction, and energy generation.

- **Energy/power:** Jensen again says energy is first-order. AI demand may make nuclear/solar/grid investment economic through private market demand rather than subsidy dependence.

- **Key risk:** narrative is promotional/event-stage. Need separate underwriting of Coherent’s margins, capex burden, capacity timing, Nvidia qualification, and whether demand/pricing stay tight as optical supply expands.

## Key Numbers / Claims

- Sherman facility: world’s first/largest volume 6-inch indium phosphide manufacturing site.
- Coherent CHIPS award: $50M.
- Nvidia/Coherent partnership: facility size to double; output to quadruple.
- Coherent founded in 1971; Jensen frames 50 years of manufacturing know-how as why this capacity is hard to replicate.
- Sherman site full capacity: 550+ direct jobs, 1,000+ direct/indirect jobs.
- AI-related US buildout claimed: ~600,000 jobs created in recent years.
- Jensen jokes facility could be 10x larger in 10 years.

## One-Line Summary

Jensen’s message: AI factories are not just GPUs — they need optical connectivity, energy, fabs, packaging, and advanced manufacturing, making Coherent’s indium phosphide lasers part of the new US AI-industrial stack.
