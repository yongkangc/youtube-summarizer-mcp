# Compare: AI Data Center Cancellation Video vs SemiAnalysis

## TL;DR

The video argues AI datacenter buildout is being quietly canceled because Nvidia / hyperscalers overestimated demand, grid components are scarce, and inventory is rising. SemiAnalysis is almost the opposite: **the “half canceled” narrative is a bad-denominator / vibe-coded forecast problem**, while their bottom-up models still show a record buildout, grid bottlenecks forcing behind-the-meter power, and demand strong enough to support multi-trillion-dollar AI capex/debt.

My view: treat the video as useful for naming the bear narrative, but weak as underwriting. SemiAnalysis has the better model stack because it ties datacenters to sites, satellite images, accelerator shipments, token demand, grid capacity, and financing structures.

## Video claims

- More than half of AI datacenter construction projects are canceled or delayed.
- US 2026 datacenter plans are allegedly half delayed/canceled.
- Oracle/OpenAI Texas expansion was postponed.
- 2025 AI infra/capex by largest tech companies was around **$400B**, exceeding US single-family housing construction.
- Nvidia / hyperscalers allegedly overestimated future AI chip demand.
- Jensen said Nvidia was distributing around **10GW worth of GPUs** in 2025.
- Goldman Sachs estimate cited: **7.7GW** of datacenters operating worldwide, used to argue Jensen’s number is exaggerated.
- Heatmap cited: **25 data contracts canceled** in last quarter of 2025; **99 of 770** planned data centers contested.
- Core bottleneck: grid/electrical components, transformers, generators, cables, tariffs, Chinese supply dependence.
- Nvidia inventory allegedly rose from close to **5M units** to **21M units**; video implies this may signal weak distribution / future oversupply.
- Gaming GPU production reduction due to DRAM shortage is framed as evidence broader supply shortages will delay AI datacenters.

## SemiAnalysis counter

### 1. “Half canceled” is exactly the narrative SemiAnalysis attacks

SemiAnalysis published **“Stop Saying Half of 2026 US Datacenter Capacity Is Canceled”** on 2026-06-18.

Key claims:

- The half-canceled claim traces back to Bloomberg’s April 2026 piece about fragile electrical-equipment supply chains, then got sharpened by TechRadar / Tom’s Hardware / The Register into clickbait.
- SemiAnalysis says these estimates use bad denominators: they treat announcements as capacity forecasts and fail to distinguish speculative megaprojects from real under-construction capacity.
- SemiAnalysis says their own model, updated site-by-site, moved only:
  - **~1%** for YE2026 North America hyperscaler self-build forecast over the prior six months.
  - **<5%** for North America colocation.
- They explicitly call some of these competing estimates “vibe-coded datacenter forecasts.”
- Their Vision Model, trained on hundreds of thousands of satellite images, says even the “under construction” figures behind the half-canceled claims are off by multiples.
- They say the **top two hyperscalers alone** exceed the 5GW “under construction” number cited by Sightline-style datasets, before including third-party developer capacity.

Read-through: the video is leaning on the exact headline SemiAnalysis says is structurally wrong.

### 2. Grid bottleneck is real, but SemiAnalysis treats it as a power-supply trade, not demand collapse

SemiAnalysis **“US Grid Constraints: Towards 40GW+ of Behind-The-Meter Datacenter by 2028?”** agrees grid constraints are severe.

But their conclusion differs:

- US datacenter buildout still rises from **+21GW in 2026** to **+84GW by 2030**.
- Behind-the-meter power likely supplies **well over half of new US datacenters in 2028+**.
- DC behind-the-meter equipment TAM crosses **50GW/year by 2029**.
- Constraint shifts demand toward onsite generation / gas / engines / fuel cells / grid-adjacent power solutions.

Read-through: grid scarcity does not automatically mean “AI buildout canceled.” It means the winners rotate toward whoever can secure power fastest and finance it.

### 3. SemiAnalysis demand-side models contradict Nvidia oversupply framing

SemiAnalysis **“Nvidia GPU Debt Backstop Unleashes the AI Project Trinity”** says:

- General market is still materially lower than SemiAnalysis’ supply-chain tracking for Nvidia H2 shipment volumes and revenue.
- AI debt financing could exceed **$7T outstanding by 2029**.
- Annual AI capex, including GPUs/networking/storage/CPU + datacenter capex, could be **well north of $2T in 2028**.
- Cumulative AI capex from 2024–2029 could reach **~$11.1T**.
- Long-term take-or-pay compute contracts make clusters financeable.

Read-through: SemiAnalysis sees demand being credit-financed and contract-backed, not broadly evaporating.

### 4. Tokenomics / model-lab news supports real demand

SemiAnalysis’ recent Anthropic work says:

- OpenAI + Anthropic combine for roughly **$100B ARR**.
- Claude Code / agentic coding is driving serious B2B monetization.
- Anthropic is framed as profitable / margin-expanding, not as a synthetic demand mirage.

This matters because token demand is the demand-side proof for compute. If token revenue and agentic workloads are compounding, the buildout is less bubble-like. If model progress stalls, the bear case gets stronger.

## Where the video is weakest

- **Confuses operational GW with accelerator shipment GW.** Live datacenter GW is not the same as annual GPU shipments or future installed compute. Comparing Jensen’s 10GW to Goldman’s 7.7GW operating base is likely apples-to-oranges.
- **Uses contested project counts without capacity weighting.** A canceled speculative site is not equivalent to a hyperscaler self-build campus already under construction.
- **Inventory inference is bad.** Nvidia inventory growth is in dollars, not “units” in standard SEC reporting. SEC company facts show inventory rose from **$10.08B** at FY2025 end to **$21.403B** at FY2026 end and **$25.797B** at Q1 FY2027. That can reflect Blackwell/rack-scale ramp, HBM/components, or cycle buffer; it is not direct evidence of unsold GPUs.
- **Gaming GPU reduction is not AI GPU demand proof.** Reducing gaming GPU production due to DRAM shortage can be consistent with AI demand crowding consumer supply, not AI oversupply.
- **Circular-deal risk is real but not proven here.** Nvidia/OpenAI financing deserves scrutiny, but SemiAnalysis frames this as a financing/backstop ecosystem issue, not automatically fake demand.

## Where the video is directionally right

- Grid/electrical equipment is a real binding constraint.
- Transformer/generator/cable lead times and tariffs can delay projects.
- Some announced projects are speculative and will be canceled or resized.
- AI infrastructure capex is now macro-relevant and financing-sensitive.
- If model progress or token demand slows, the entire chain derates hard.

## Best synthesis

SemiAnalysis model stack says the right question is not “are datacenters canceled?” but:

- Which announced projects are real vs speculative?
- Which sites have power, permits, construction progress, and firm offtake?
- Which clusters have take-or-pay contracts and credible financing?
- Does token revenue justify GPU TCO?
- Where is the next bottleneck: GPU, HBM, networking, CPU, grid, transformer, gas turbine, BTM power, debt capacity?

## Trade / investment read-through

- **Do not short Nvidia just because of this video.** The video’s strongest evidence is mostly headline aggregation and weak denominator math.
- **Better short basket:** speculative datacenter developers / neoclouds with unfunded campuses, weak offtake, no power certainty, or debt-dependent economics.
- **Better long basket if SemiAnalysis right:** Nvidia, HBM/memory, networking, power equipment, behind-the-meter generation, electrical infrastructure, and high-quality datacenter builders with secured power.
- **Key spread:** real power-secured / contract-backed capacity vs press-release capacity.
- **Kill test for SemiAnalysis bull case:** if token demand / model-lab revenue decelerates while capex and AI debt keep rising, then the video’s bubble frame becomes more dangerous.

## One-line summary

The video says “AI datacenters are being canceled because demand was overestimated”; SemiAnalysis says “the cancellation narrative is bad modeling — the real story is power/grid bottlenecks plus financed compute demand still scaling hard.”
