# 2026 Nvidia Annual Stockholder Meeting

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VMxiSvTFf8o

## TL;DR

Jensen’s core message: AI has shifted computing from retrieving information to manufacturing intelligence, and NVIDIA is selling the infrastructure for that new production system. The company frames demand as durable because useful AI tokens can generate revenue, with the next legs being agentic AI, inference, sovereign/enterprise AI factories, and physical AI.

## Key Insights

- Jensen frames AI as a computing reset larger than prior platform shifts.
  - Mainframe to PC, PC to internet, internet/cloud/mobile, then AI.
  - Old model: humans write software, computers execute instructions.
  - New model: computers reason, plan, use tools, call other agents, and do work.

- The key metaphor is the “AI factory.”
  - Traditional data centers store and serve files.
  - AI factories manufacture tokens.
  - Tokens become code, answers, designs, actions, and services.
  - His investor framing: useful AI is profitable, and every token is a unit of potential profit.

- Agentic AI is the demand accelerator.
  - Agents can use tools, access memory, write code, call other agents, test results, and keep working until the task is done.
  - Coding is presented as the first major enterprise breakout use case.
  - Jensen argues the AI ROI question is answered because AI is now doing useful work.

- NVIDIA’s FY numbers were very strong.
  - Revenue grew **65%** to **$216B**.
  - Operating income rose **60%** to **$130B**.
  - Diluted EPS increased **67%** to **$4.90**.
  - Operating cash flow was **$103B**.
  - Returned **$41B** to shareholders.
  - Data center revenue was **$194B**, up **68%**.

- Blackwell is framed as the inference economics leader.
  - Jensen cites SemiAnalysis InferenceX, saying Blackwell delivers the lowest cost per token and **30x higher token throughput** than the next-best platform.
  - Inference matters because it is the monetization layer of AI: generating tokens for paying use cases.
  - NVIDIA argues its systems may not be cheapest to buy, but they produce the lowest-cost and highest-revenue tokens.

- Vera Rubin is positioned as the platform for agents.
  - Hopper was for pre-training.
  - Blackwell brought inference to rack scale.
  - Vera Rubin is built for agentic AI.
  - Jensen’s point: agents stress the full system because they think, retrieve memory, execute code, call apps, and loop repeatedly.
  - If CPUs bottleneck, expensive GPUs sit idle, which means lost AI-factory revenue.

- Vera is NVIDIA’s new CPU market thesis.
  - “Vera is the CPU for agents. Rubin is the GPU for thinking.”
  - Jensen says previous CPUs were built for humans and rented per core.
  - Agents operate at nanosecond latency and need CPUs built for ultra-fast responses.
  - He calls Vera one of the most significant product launches in NVIDIA history.

- Networking remains a major moat.
  - NVIDIA says it has three networking businesses:
    - NVLink scale-up inside racks.
    - Spectrum-X Ethernet for AI scale-out and across AI factories.
    - InfiniBand for low-latency AI and scientific systems.
  - Jensen claims Spectrum-X is now larger than all other Ethernet networking peers combined.

- CUDA is presented as the compounding software flywheel.
  - Installed base attracts developers.
  - Developers create applications.
  - Applications create new markets.
  - New markets expand the installed base.
  - CUDA-X libraries are described as NVIDIA’s “crown jewels,” now becoming agent tools for domains like lithography, genomics, physics, robotics, radio networks, and drug discovery.

- NVIDIA is reframing itself into two broad market platforms.
  - Data center:
    - Hyperscale and AI cloud.
    - Industrial and enterprise.
  - Edge computing:
    - PCs, workstations, gaming, AI base stations, robotics, and automotive.

- Physical AI is the next growth wave.
  - Physical AI means agentic AI in the real world: robots, cars, factories, surgical robotics, hospitality, and industrial systems.
  - NVIDIA’s stack: AI factories train models, Omniverse simulates them, chips/computers run them, and Cosmos provides world foundation models.

## Q&A Highlights

- On sustainability of the AI buildout:
  - NVIDIA says the buildout will be measured in decades and compares it to the electric grid, transportation systems, and the internet.
  - They call it potentially the largest infrastructure buildout in human history.
  - Demand should expand beyond clouds into enterprises, sovereign nations, regional AI clouds, and physical AI.

- On GPUs for inference:
  - NVIDIA argues GPUs remain preferred because data centers are power-limited, so performance per watt determines token throughput and revenue.
  - They cite SemiAnalysis, MLPerf inference wins, and Artificial Analysis agent benchmarks.
  - Claim: Grace Blackwell 300 NVLink 72 can run up to **20x more agents per megawatt** than Hopper.

- On frontier AI and national security:
  - NVIDIA argues supporting frontier systems strengthens US technology leadership, jobs, reindustrialization, and national security.
  - It names partners including Intel, Micron, TSMC, Wistron, Foxconn, Ampere, Marvell, Corning, Coherent, and Lumentum.
  - Also mentions power/grid partners like Caterpillar, GE Vernova, Vertiv, nuclear, and renewable energy players.

- On export controls and China:
  - NVIDIA says national security comes first and it complies with US export controls.
  - It says smuggled AI data centers are impractical because advanced systems need trusted hardware, software, networking, and support.
  - The US has approved H200 licenses for China-based customers, but NVIDIA says it has not generated revenue yet and is uncertain whether imports will be allowed into China.

- On capital returns:
  - NVIDIA announced a **25x increase** in quarterly dividend.
  - It authorized an additional **$80B** in share repurchases.
  - It plans to return **50% or more of free cash flow** this year, next year, and beyond.

## Investor Read-Through

- NVIDIA is explicitly trying to shift investor focus from “GPU units sold” to “AI factory economics”: cost per token, tokens per watt, throughput, and revenue per data center.
- The deepest moat claim is full-stack co-design: GPU, CPU, networking, software, CUDA libraries, systems, and ecosystem.
- The next underwriting question is not whether AI is real, but whether token demand, agentic workflows, and physical AI can absorb the coming infrastructure supply at attractive economics.
- Supplier read-through is strong for optical/networking, memory, power, cooling, advanced packaging, and US-anchored AI infrastructure partners.

## One-Line Summary

NVIDIA’s 2026 meeting frames AI as a decades-long infrastructure supercycle: AI factories manufacture profitable tokens, Blackwell/Rubin improve inference and agent economics, and NVIDIA wants to own the full stack from data center to physical AI.
