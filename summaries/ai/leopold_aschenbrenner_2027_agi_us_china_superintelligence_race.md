# Leopold Aschenbrenner — 2027 AGI, China/US super-intelligence race, & the return of history

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zdbVtZIn9IM

## TL;DR

Leopold’s thesis: AGI is not a vague sci-fi future anymore — it is becoming an **industrial, geopolitical race** driven by compute, power, data centers, algorithmic secrets, and state security. He thinks by **2027–2028**, models may become expert-level “drop-in remote workers,” then rapidly accelerate AI research itself, creating a superintelligence race between the US and China.

## Core thesis

AI is no longer just software.

It is becoming an industrial process:

- giant GPU clusters
- huge power plants
- massive capex
- chip supply chains
- eventually fabs and national mobilization

His frame: the next stage of AI may look less like “Silicon Valley app cycle” and more like **Manhattan Project + Cold War + industrial war mobilization**.

## The trillion-dollar cluster

He describes a scaling path:

- GPT-4 cluster: roughly **$500M** total build cost, not just rental compute
- 2024/2025: multi-billion-dollar clusters
- 2027/2028: around **10 GW** clusters, possibly true AGI range
- 2030: possible **100 GW / trillion-dollar cluster**
  - 100M H100-equivalent GPUs
  - over **20% of US electricity production**
  - hundreds of billions to trillion-dollar scale

His point: this becomes too big for ordinary startup governance. It becomes a state-level strategic asset.

## Model capability timeline

He argues the next jumps come from two things:

- **Scaling**
  - Training compute keeps growing ~0.5 orders of magnitude per year.
  - From GPT-2 to GPT-4 was “preschooler to smart high schooler.”
  - Another similar jump on top of GPT-4 by 2027/2028 could be enormous.

- **Unhobbling**
  - Current models are smart but constrained as chatbots.
  - They don’t yet use computers well, run long tasks, self-correct reliably, or think for millions of tokens.
  - If unlocked, they become more like autonomous agents / remote workers.

His claim:

- 2025–2026: models smarter than most college graduates
- 2027–2028: models as smart as top experts
- then possibly a fast jump to superintelligence if AI automates AI research

## “Drop-in remote worker” idea

He says intermediate models may already be useful but require too much workflow integration.

The killer transition is when AI becomes:

- reliable enough
- agentic enough
- able to use computers
- able to do long-horizon tasks
- cheap enough to deploy at scale

Then instead of “software engineer + Copilot,” you may just have “AI software engineer.”

That is when revenue, capex, national security attention, and political reaction all accelerate.

## Intelligence explosion mechanism

His core acceleration story:

- AGI can automate AI research.
- You could run millions of AI researchers in parallel.
- That compresses a decade of ML progress into maybe a year.
- Then those AI systems improve robotics, science, weapons, cyber, manufacturing, etc.
- Result: superintelligence becomes a national-power discontinuity.

This is why he thinks it is not just another technology.

## China / US race

He expects China to treat this as existential once capabilities become obvious.

Main claims:

- CCP will try to infiltrate US AI labs.
- Espionage effort could involve billions of dollars and thousands of people.
- China can build power infrastructure much faster than the US.
- If China steals model weights or key algorithmic secrets, it may catch up quickly.
- A tight race creates incentives to cut corners on safety and deploy dangerous systems too fast.

His key concern: **neck-and-neck AGI race is extremely unstable**.

A 6–24 month US lead could matter enormously because:

- gives time for alignment/safety work
- reduces panic-race dynamics
- prevents China from immediately copying or overtaking
- gives democracies more control over the transition

## Why model security matters

He says current AI lab security is far too weak.

Example mentioned:

- A Google AI employee allegedly stole code by copying it into Apple Notes and exporting a PDF.
- DeepMind’s own frontier security framework reportedly rates current security around level zero, while state-actor resistance would require much higher levels.

His point:

- GPT-4 weights being stolen today is not catastrophic.
- AGI/superintelligence weights being stolen in 2027 could be catastrophic.
- Security upgrades take years, so labs need to start now.

## Algorithmic secrets may matter more than compute

He says people focus too much on compute and underrate “secrets”:

- how to get past the data wall
- synthetic data methods
- RL/self-play methods
- long-horizon agent training
- unhobbling techniques
- post-training recipes

If the US keeps these secret, it may preserve a major lead.

If everything leaks or is published, China can copy faster.

He uses the Manhattan Project analogy:

- Fermi almost published graphite-moderator results.
- Szilard pushed for secrecy.
- Germany missed a key path partly because it lacked that info.
- Small scientific secrets can create huge strategic gaps.

## Middle East data-center concern

He argues advanced AGI clusters should be in:

- the US
- close allied democracies

Not in authoritarian states.

His line: **“Would you do the Manhattan Project in the UAE?”**

Concern:

- if UAE / Middle Eastern states host huge clusters, they get leverage over AGI
- compute can be seized, pressured, copied, or politically bargained with
- authoritarian regimes should not get “seats at the AGI table”

He accepts broader benefit-sharing with non-democracies, but says frontier AGI development should be controlled by a narrow democratic coalition.

## Energy / industrial policy

He says the US can build this domestically if it gets serious.

Possible paths:

- build near natural gas:
  - West Texas
  - Pennsylvania / Marcellus Shale
- deregulate / streamline green buildout:
  - solar
  - batteries
  - grid connections
  - rights of way
- use emergency/state capacity where needed

He rejects the idea that only autocracies can mobilize. He thinks betting against America is a mistake, but America needs to act like the stakes are real.

## “Return of history”

This is the philosophical part.

Leopold thinks people in liberal democracies have forgotten what high-stakes history feels like:

- war
- dictatorship
- famine
- industrial mobilization
- espionage
- regime competition

He argues AGI could bring that back.

His German family history shapes this:

- mother from East Germany
- father from West Germany
- great-grandmother lived through Nazi Germany, Dresden firebombing, Soviet occupation, Stasi dictatorship, then reunification
- this makes dictatorship and historical rupture feel concrete to him, not abstract

His warning: superintelligence could empower totalitarianism far beyond anything in the 20th century.

Potential dictatorship tools:

- perfect surveillance
- loyal automated security forces
- lie detection
- no popular uprisings
- no internal dissent
- no Gorbachev-style reformer slipping through

## Lab employees’ window of power

He says AI lab employees have unusual power now, but that power may rapidly depreciate.

Analogy: Oppenheimer scientists built the bomb, then the bomb was taken over by generals and politicians.

His warning to lab staff:

- you have influence now
- use it wisely
- later, national security states and corporate/state leaders may take over

## Practical investor / operator read

If you believe Leopold’s frame, the key bottlenecks/assets are:

- power
- GPUs / accelerators
- data centers
- cooling
- natural gas / grid infrastructure
- AI lab security
- sovereign compute policy
- frontier model companies
- chip supply chain
- inference economics
- national-security regulation

But the investment caveat: this is a **power-law / geopolitical thesis**, not a clean near-term valuation model. Timing and regulation risk are huge.

## My read

Leopold is probably directionally right that AI is becoming an industrial/geopolitical race, not just SaaS. The aggressive part is the timeline and discontinuity: 2027 AGI + rapid intelligence explosion depends on unhobbling, long-horizon agency, and AI research automation actually working.

So the crux is not “will models get better?” They will.

The crux is:

- can they become reliable autonomous workers?
- can they materially accelerate AI R&D?
- can scaling continue through power/chip constraints?
- can labs secure weights/secrets before state actors care?
- can democratic states coordinate before panic mode?

## One-line summary

Leopold argues AGI is becoming a state-level industrial arms race, and by 2027 the decisive bottlenecks may be compute, power, secrecy, and whether democracies can stay ahead of China without destroying themselves in the race.
