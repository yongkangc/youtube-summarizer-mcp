# Warren Buffett: Stop Chasing Salary and Build Character

## TLDR

Buffett gives two linked lessons: work for people you admire instead of optimizing salary/resume prestige, and deliberately build character traits that make others want to bet on you. Intelligence and energy matter, but without integrity they become dangerous.

## Career choice

- Join an institution or person you genuinely admire.
- Do not stack prestigious “in-between” jobs merely because they improve a résumé or starting salary.
- Delaying desired work indefinitely is irrational; start moving toward it now.
- Best career compounds skills, relationships and identity while remaining enjoyable enough to sustain.

Buffett's line: choosing work you dislike now so you can enjoy life later resembles “saving up sex for old age.”

## The 10% exercise

Imagine choosing one classmate and receiving 10% of their lifetime earnings. You likely would not select based on grades, appearance or inherited wealth. You would choose traits such as:

- integrity;
- honesty;
- generosity;
- initiative;
- reliability;
- willingness to contribute more than required.

Then imagine shorting one classmate and paying 10% of their lifetime earnings. Likely negative traits:

- dishonesty;
- selfishness;
- claiming others' credit;
- cutting corners;
- unreliability.

## Hiring rule

Buffett seeks three qualities:

1. Intelligence
2. Energy
3. Integrity

Without integrity, intelligence and energy amplify damage. His joke: if someone lacks integrity, better that they be lazy and unintelligent.

## Habits compound

Character is largely chosen through repeated behavior. Buffett quotes:

> “The chains of habit are too light to be felt until they are too heavy to be broken.”

At a young age, identify admirable qualities, practice them deliberately and remove destructive traits before they harden. Ben Franklin and Ben Graham reportedly used this imitation process: identify whom you admire, understand why, then behave accordingly.

## Caveat

Buffett underweights practical constraints. Salary, dependants, immigration, healthcare and runway can make an interim job rational. Correct lesson is not “ignore money”; it is:

> Meet survival and optionality needs, then optimize for admired people, learning and work you would still choose—not external résumé status.

## YK read

This is internal-scorecard advice. Build life around people and work you respect; let income emerge from long-term capability and trust rather than treating prestige as objective.

For one-man hedge fund process, person worth “buying 10% of” is not trader with hottest month. It is operator with structural edge, audited decisions, honesty about losses, disciplined risk and enough integrity to remain trusted through drawdowns.

## Bottom line

Choose work that compounds who you want to become. Build intelligence and energy, but make integrity control both.
