# All-In's Best Ideas Pitch Competition: 4 Investors Present Their Top Trades Live

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fO5sC7qS04E

## TL;DR

All-In runs a live Sohn-style “best ideas” pitch competition. Four investors pitch MGM Resorts, Talon Energy, Actis Oncology, and Geonet. The useful investor takeaway: MGM and Talon are the more sizeable public-equity ideas with downside protection; Actis and Geonet are higher-variance lottery-ticket style ideas.

## Ideas pitched

### 1. MGM Resorts — casino SOTP with hidden Japan/Dubai options

Pitcher: Aaron Cowen

Core thesis:
- MGM is being valued mainly as a Vegas/Macau casino company, but has underappreciated embedded options.
- Barry Diller owns ~26% and bid ~$48/share; presenter says he would not tender because upside is much higher.
- MGM and the company have bought back huge amounts of stock; presenter says rarely has he seen a company buy back about half its float over six years.

Valuation pieces:
- Vegas + China assets: low-$60s/share.
- Osaka, Japan casino license: potentially another ~$50/share.
- Dubai casino option: potentially another ~$40–50/share if gambling is legalized.
- Presenter’s target: stock could be worth **$100–150/share**, roughly a double/triple depending on entry.

Japan logic:
- Osaka is the only Japanese prefecture moving forward with an integrated resort/casino.
- Japan already has a large gambling-adjacent market: pachinko + horse racing around ~$40B, vs Macau ~$30B and Vegas ~$10B.
- MGM owns 40% of the Osaka property and earns a management fee.
- Osaka is accessible to Chinese gamblers and is in a first-world jurisdiction, so the asset may deserve a higher multiple than Macau.

Dubai logic:
- MGM has a branded Dubai complex with MGM/Aria/Bellagio-style assets and reportedly ~300k sq ft of reserved space.
- Gambling is illegal in Dubai today, but Wynn is opening in Ras Al Khaimah; if Dubai legalizes, MGM’s location becomes a major embedded option.

Risks:
- Dubai option may never happen.
- Osaka opens around 2030, so timing matters.
- Casino cycle and consumer weakness remain risks.

### 2. Talon Energy — scarce power for AI data centers

Pitcher: Daniel Dreyfus

Core thesis:
- AI creates a power-cycle shock. If you can buy scarce hard assets below replacement cost when demand is about to explode, you can make a lot of money.
- Talon owns ~2GW nuclear and ~6GW natural-gas baseload power.
- It trades around **$25B EV** vs estimated **$45B replacement cost**.
- Because of debt, equity upside to replacement cost is more than a double.

Macro frame:
- US power demand used to grow roughly with GDP.
- AI, robotics, and data centers create a step-change in electricity demand.
- China massively outbuilt the US in power generation; the US needs to “copy China” and build.
- Data centers/hyperscalers are desperate for long-term PPAs.

Key examples:
- Microsoft/Constellation Three Mile Island restart: Microsoft reportedly agreed to pay ~$100/MWh for 20 years vs power prices around ~$50/MWh to incentivize restart.
- Regulators are increasingly worried that data centers may raise consumer power bills.

Valuation math:
- Stock in high-$300s.
- Do-nothing case: ~$50/share annual FCF once Amazon data-center contract rolls up; at ~15x infrastructure multiple, roughly double potential.
- More data-center contracts / higher power prices: ~$70/share recurring FCF; at 15x, about ~$1,050/share.
- Build 4GW of the ~100GW needed: could exceed ~$100/share FCF.

Risks:
- Regulatory backlash against data centers consuming grid power.
- Political price caps or utility intervention.
- Interest-rate sensitivity because infrastructure multiples depend on discount rates.

### 3. Actis Oncology / AKTS — radiopharmaceutical platform

Pitcher: Oleg Nodelman

Core thesis:
- Biotech is usually a casino; EcoR1 tries to play poker by focusing on risk/reward and margin of safety.
- Actis is a radiopharmaceutical oncology company using mini-protein targeting to deliver radioactive payloads precisely to tumors.

Company facts from pitch:
- Ticker: **AKTS**.
- Market cap: ~$1B.
- Enterprise value: ~$500M.
- Cash runway: >3 years, beyond key milestones.
- Recent $300M IPO was 18x oversubscribed and backed by a $100M Eli Lilly order.

Science thesis:
- Radiopharmaceuticals are like “micro drones” in the bloodstream: target tumor cells, deliver radiation with very small blast radius, minimize collateral damage.
- Imaging can verify target engagement early, de-risking development.
- First targets are known/validated: Nectin-4 in bladder cancer and B7H3 across major solid tumors including prostate, colorectal, and lung.
- Initial clinical data guided for 2027, with Nectin-4 potentially as early as Q1.

Upside:
- If one program works, market may value the whole mini-protein platform.
- Pharma appetite is high: Bristol, Novartis, Bayer, Lilly and others building radiotherapy capability.
- Presenter says Actis could be worth **~$10B / ~$200 per share** if one program reaches market.

Risks:
- Classic biotech binary readout risk.
- Bad data could mean 50–75% downside.
- Competitive modalities may emerge.
- China-copying risk may be lower because radioisotope supply chain is harder, but not zero.

### 4. Geonet / GEOD — crypto token for precision location network

Pitcher: Kyle Samani

Core thesis:
- Geonet is a decentralized RTK network for centimeter-level location, positioned as “rails for physical AI.”
- Robotics, drones, autonomous farming, AV mapping, and physical AI need more precise location than GPS.

What it does:
- GPS gives roughly ~2m precision.
- RTK gives roughly ~2cm precision, about 100x more accurate.
- Geonet crowdsources base stations: people buy a few-hundred-dollar base station, place it on a roof, broadcast radio signals, and earn GEOD tokens.

Network stats from pitch:
- Founded 2021; began network buildout 2022.
- Claims to be the world’s largest and fastest-growing RTK network.
- Roughly twice the size of Trimble + Hexagon + Topcon combined.
- Live in ~150 countries, >11k cities, covering ~80% of global population excluding sanctioned countries.

Customers/use cases:
- Agriculture and robotic mules.
- John Deere / unmanned spraying systems.
- TomTom mapping for AV programs.
- Drones/robotics/physical AI generally.

Business/token economics:
- ARR crossed about ~$11M, growing >3x YoY.
- 80% of revenue reportedly used to buy GEOD tokens on the open market; visible on Solana.
- Fully diluted token valuation around ~$150M; crypto sites may show ~$60–70M due to float.
- Presenter says token, not equity, is the exposure because the company has a contractual relationship with Geonet Foundation to use revenue to buy tokens.

Risks:
- Crypto/governance complexity.
- Satellite-based centimeter location could eventually compete.
- Gavin and others were skeptical because they do not invest in crypto.

## Judging / rankings

Audience vote:
- 1st: Talon Energy — 50%
- 2nd: MGM — 24%
- 3rd: Actis — 21%
- 4th: Geonet — 5%

Bestie vote:
- 1st: MGM
- 2nd: Talon
- 3rd: Actis
- 4th: Geonet

Gavin Baker’s read:
- MGM looked best on pure risk/reward because Barry Diller’s bid caps downside while Japan/Dubai add upside.
- Talon is compelling, but regulatory risk around AI power demand is hard to size.
- Actis is interesting if it becomes a platform, but he is not a healthcare investor.
- Geonet is fascinating, but he does not play crypto.

## My investor read

- **MGM**: cleanest risk/reward. Existing bid + buybacks + hidden assets + clear SOTP. Best “could size it” idea.
- **Talon**: most tied to AI infrastructure theme. Scarce power is real, but politics/regulation can confiscate some upside.
- **Actis**: classic asymmetric biotech. Interesting setup, but data risk dominates.
- **Geonet**: interesting physical-AI infrastructure idea, but token/equity/governance path makes it much harder to underwrite.

## One-line summary

The episode’s best practical ideas are MGM for event-driven SOTP upside and Talon for AI-power scarcity; Actis and Geonet are more venture-style asymmetric bets.
