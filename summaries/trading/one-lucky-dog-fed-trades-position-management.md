# Scott's Best Trades and Trade-Management Framework

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RNyE7OUiX1U

## TL;DR

Main value is portfolio management, not featured trade picks: reduce size first when uncomfortable; close when IV edge is gone, thesis changed, or capital use is poor; otherwise adjust untested side, recenter, or roll out. Fed releases create short-lived volatility but their interpretation is not reliably predictable.

## Fed / macro releases

- CPI, FOMC minutes, and testimony matter mainly because they create tradable volatility.
- Knowing number does not reveal market reaction; even company executives cannot reliably predict stock response to their own earnings.
- Edge is not forecasting headline. It is providing liquidity / selling inflated volatility after reaction when compensation exceeds tail risk.

## Featured trades

These are presenters' examples, not validated recommendations:

- AMD: August `690/710` short call spread after rally; IVR `94`, POP `82%`. Thesis: stock overextended. Main risk: momentum/upside squeeze.
- Oracle: August `115/110` short put spread for about `$2.20`; IVR `52`, POP `76%`. Thesis: severe selloff / mean reversion.
- Euro futures: September short `112` put for `28 ticks` = about `$350`; IVR `60`, POP `72%`.
- Rocket Lab: naked `65` put; IVR `60`, POP `72%`, breakeven reportedly just over `61`. High gap/notional risk despite willingness to own shares.

## When uncomfortable with a position

1. **Close** when:
   - IVR collapsed, e.g. `95 → 45`, so vol-sale edge harvested.
   - Original directional/vol thesis changed.
   - Position is too risky or capital-inefficient.
   - Example: tying up `$3k–4k` for remaining `$10` premium is poor risk/reward.
2. **Reduce size first** when discomfort impairs judgment. Fastest way to lower delta, margin, and emotional pressure.
3. **Adjust untested side** in same expiry:
   - Challenged short put: sell/roll down call.
   - Challenged short call: roll up put.
   - Collect premium and reduce net delta, but recognize gross risk can rise.
4. **Recenter** by closing current strangle and opening new strikes around spot in one multi-leg order.
5. **Roll out** to next monthly expiry and usually recenter. More duration lowers near-term delta/gamma, but does not erase loss or tail exposure.

## Other market views

- KOSPI selloff/circuit breakers may affect overnight US futures because global desks hedge with liquid US markets, but offer little retail lead because US prices absorb information continuously.
- Dow is mostly financial theater versus S&P 500: price-weighted, weak benchmark relevance, low derivatives interest relative to SPX.
- Naked puts should be sized by buying power/notional, not contract count; a `$500` stock needs much smaller size than a `$50` stock.

## Caveats

- Rolling/adjusting changes exposure; it does not magically reduce economic loss.
- Rolling untested side reduces delta but adds opposite-side tail risk and narrows safe range.
- “Roll out lowers risk 20–30%” is platform-delta framing, not universal portfolio-risk reduction.
- Featured trades rely mostly on high IVR/mean reversion. Structural edge is short-vol/liquidity provision; kill test is persistent trend, gap, or correlation shock.

## One-line summary

Trade events for volatility, not prediction; when position hurts, cut size first and only adjust when remaining vol premium still pays for added tail risk.
