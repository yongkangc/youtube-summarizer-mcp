# Managing the $100K+ Options Portfolio

## TL;DR

Roll/recenter near 21 DTE to reduce delta drift, correlation, capital use and portfolio volatility. In model update, management lowered beta-weighted directional exposure, released over 10% capital and improved probability of profit on four positions.

## Exact update

- Model equity: **$100k**; capital used initially about **$47.5k–$48k**.
- Views: bullish SPY/FXI/euro/BITO, bearish QQQ/XOP, neutral IWM.
- Market since entry: roughly **-3.1%**; volatility roughly **+6.3%**.
- Rolled September to October just before **21 DTE** and recentered toward original delta.
- Added credit: about **$4,410**.
- Post-adjustment SPY beta-weighted delta: about **-60**; one week later around **+28**.
- Correlation to SPY improved from **0.45 to 0.35**.
- Probability of profit improved for IWM, euro, XOP and BITO.
- Capital use fell from about **$48k to $43k**.
- Presenters estimated about **25% lower portfolio volatility** versus no adjustment.

## Practical rules

- Prefer rolling 1–2 days before 21 DTE rather than after.
- Recenter tested positions only when thesis still valid.
- Weight positions by capital and beta-weighted delta.
- Keep directional portfolio delta small relative to account.
- Evaluate portfolio correlation, not ticker count.
- Use released capital as reserve; do not automatically redeploy.

## Caveat

Rolling for credit does not remove loss. Track total campaign cash flow, remaining tail risk and new duration. Short-vol edge is compensation for convexity/gap risk, not free theta.

## One-line summary

Mechanical 21-DTE management can reduce path risk and free capital, but only campaign-level P&L reveals whether risk was improved or merely deferred.
