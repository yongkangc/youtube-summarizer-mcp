# Diversification Isn't Always The Crash Insurance I Thought It Was

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N0BqftwThjg

## TL;DR

Tastytrade’s research point: equity diversification works least when you need it most. In market stress, correlations between individual equities/sector ETFs and SPY rise sharply, so risk control may require cutting net market exposure rather than assuming a basket of stocks will hedge itself.

## Key points

- Common crash pattern: “all correlations go to one” is broadly true for equities during sharp selloffs.
- Apple vs SPY normally had about **0.61** long-term correlation, but after the April 2 pullback it jumped to about **0.86**.
- Using VIX as the stress gauge, Apple/SPY correlation rose as volatility increased:
  - VIX > 20: about **0.50**
  - VIX > 30: about **0.65**
  - VIX > 40: about **0.80**
- They checked more than tech: Southwest/LUV, emerging markets/EEM, Apple, XLE, and Costco.
- Across these names/ETFs, correlations to SPY rose materially when VIX moved above 20/30/40.
- Average correlation moved from roughly **0.66** to **0.84** in high-volatility regimes.
- Practical conclusion: traditional equity diversification becomes much less effective during volatility spikes.
- Risk management implication: in high-vol regimes, reduce overall market exposure; don’t rely only on owning “different” equities.
- Tony’s caveat: this was all equity exposure. Futures/asset-class diversification may offer more genuinely uncorrelated exposures, though that needs separate testing.

## Trading read-through

The edge/risk lens: equity baskets still carry common beta and crash correlation. If your “hedge” is just another equity or equity-sector ETF, it probably becomes delta in disguise during stress. Real crash insurance needs explicit convexity, lower net beta, cross-asset hedges, or pre-defined de-risking rules.

## One-line summary

Diversification inside equities is not crash insurance; when VIX spikes, common beta dominates and position sizing matters more than basket construction.
