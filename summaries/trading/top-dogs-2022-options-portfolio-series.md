## Series TL;DR

Build a short-premium portfolio from many small, liquid, weakly correlated positions. Allocate by buying-power usage, beta-weight every position to SPY, retain roughly half account as reserve, roll/recenter around 21 DTE, and scale by adding underlyings plus contracts rather than concentrating into one large position.

Theory of edge: earn volatility risk premium while diversifying directional views and keeping unused liquidity. Risk being warehoused: short volatility, negative convexity, gaps, correlation convergence and expanding margin during stress.

## Episode 1 — Initial construction

Model account:

- Equity: **$100,000**.
- Buying power used: roughly **$43k–$50k**.
- Entry: liquid products around **45 DTE**.
- Products: `SPY`, `QQQ`, `IWM`, `FXI`, euro futures/options, `XOP`, `BITO`.
- Views: bullish SPY, bearish QQQ, neutral IWM, bullish China/euro/Bitcoin, bearish energy.
- Structures: short puts, short calls, short strangles and covered call.
- Initial premium/extrinsic: roughly **$7,000** in earlier model snapshot.
- Theta: about **$189/day**, or **0.189% of account**.
- SPY beta-weighted delta: about **+113**, roughly $47k SPY-equivalent long notional at then-current SPY.
- Estimated correlation: **0.30**.

Key lesson: raw deltas across products cannot be added meaningfully. Convert to SPY-equivalent beta-weighted delta first.

## Episode 2 — Management update

Portfolio stayed under **$50k capital use** and was intentionally directional but diversified across views.

Market since entry:

- SPY approximately **-3.1%**.
- Volatility approximately **+6.3%**.
- Both moves challenged short-vol, slightly long portfolio.

Management performed:

- Rolled September positions into October just before **21 DTE**; presenters prefer 1–2 days early rather than late.
- Recentered positions toward original deltas.
- Collected about **$4,410 additional credit**.
- Post-roll SPY beta-weighted delta: approximately **-60**, small versus their normal 200–400 delta directional range for $100k account.
- Weekly drift later moved portfolio to roughly **+28 delta**.
- Correlation improved from **0.45 to 0.35**.
- Probability of profit improved on IWM, euro, XOP and BITO.
- Capital use fell from about **$48k to roughly $43k**, over 10% reduction.
- Claimed portfolio volatility improved about **25%** versus leaving positions unmanaged.

Management rule: manage position risk first, use rolls/recentering to restore probability and delta, then reassess portfolio.

Important caveat: rolling does not erase economic loss. Added credit and lower displayed delta can defer or reshape risk; campaign-level realized/unrealized P&L still matters.

## Episode 3 — Scaling up

After five weeks:

- Neutral/index trades worked better than directional views.
- Correct directional view: bearish crude/energy.
- Wrong views included China, euro and Bitcoin.
- Despite market decline and higher VIX, portfolio P&L was described as near flat because positions were offset and managed.

Scaling methods:

- Increase contracts in existing positions.
- Add genuinely different underlyings.
- Add strategies/directions.
- Widen strikes where appropriate.
- Do not concentrate scaling into one or two large SPX positions.

New exposure:

- Added to `SPY`, `QQQ`, `IWM`.
- Added `TLT` strangles, `NVDA` short puts, `FSLR` short calls, `EWZ` short calls and `JPM` strangles.
- Five new underlyings plus additions to three existing positions.
- Capital use rose from roughly **$43k to $84.3k**, framed as scaling model toward a **$200k** account.
- SPY beta-weighted delta changed only from about **+62 to +45**.
- Estimated portfolio correlation stayed near **0.35**.
- Additional monthly premium: roughly **$7,900**.
- Total extrinsic increased from about **$15k to $22k**.
- Their target was retaining roughly **25%**, or **$5k–$6k**, but they explicitly said this is an expectation, not guaranteed monthly result.

## What series gets right

- Portfolio construction matters more than finding one “best” ticker.
- Beta-weighted delta exposes hidden aggregate direction.
- Half-sized initial deployment leaves room for volatility expansion and defense.
- Position size should respect smallest tradable contract and buying power.
- Scaling via more independent bets is safer than multiplying one bet.
- 21 DTE reduces gamma/path risk and creates management consistency.
- Neutral positions can outperform directional opinions when forecasts are wrong.

## What series understates

- Buying-power reduction is not maximum loss for undefined-risk options.
- Historical correlation often rises sharply in crises.
- A 25% premium-retention target is not stable yield.
- Rolling credit can hide accumulated campaign losses.
- Short calls/puts/strangles remain negatively convex; small normal-period delta can explode after large moves.
- Using **84%** of account buying power in a scaled short-vol portfolio leaves little stress buffer unless account truly doubled to $200k as presenters assume.
- Portfolio vega, gamma, concentration and liquidity deserve explicit limits alongside delta/theta.

## Operational version

1. Define account risk budget and minimum reserve.
2. Start around 35–50% buying-power use, not full deployment.
3. Use liquid index/ETF/futures products around 45 DTE.
4. Mix bullish, bearish and neutral positions across distinct economic drivers.
5. Track SPY beta-weighted delta, theta, vega, gamma, BPR and stress correlation.
6. At/near 21 DTE, close or roll; evaluate campaign economics, not roll credit alone.
7. Recenter only when thesis and expected value remain valid.
8. Scale through more independent positions before more contracts.
9. Stress test equity selloff + vol spike + correlation-to-one + margin expansion.
10. Treat theta as compensation for tail risk, never income guarantee.

## One-line summary

Diversify short-premium risk across products and views, manage around 21 DTE, and scale breadth before size—but judge safety under crisis correlation and convexity, not normal-period buying power or theta.
