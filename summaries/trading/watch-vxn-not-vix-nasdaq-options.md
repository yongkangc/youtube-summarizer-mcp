# Most Traders Watch VIX. The Head of Nasdaq's Index Options Content Says Watch VXN

📺 Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qw_nuaqJiIk

## TL;DR

Kevin Davitt argues VXN/NDX vol is the better risk gauge than VIX when market risk is concentrated in mega-cap tech, semis, AI, and Nasdaq-heavy single names. Main trade idea: NDX volatility is priced much richer than SPX, so a relative-vol/downside structure can sell expensive NDX downside while buying cheaper SPX downside, but the risk is a highly correlated selloff.

## Key Insights

- **Nasdaq 100 is where current risk clusters** — top retail/trader focus names like TQQQ, SOXL, Tesla, Micron, Intel, AMD, SpaceX-linked appetite, and semi/memory themes all tie directly or indirectly to Nasdaq 100 dynamics.
- **Not 2000-style bubble claim** — Davitt says today differs from dot-com because major index constituents are profitable and cash-generative. Pullbacks can happen, but catalyst is not obvious “cascading risk lower.”
- **SK Hynix / Korean memory matters** — he flags SK Hynix ADR/US-market access as important because Korean memory/semi moves increasingly explain US pre-market action in Micron, semis, SOXL, TQQQ, and broader Nasdaq risk.
- **NDX options volume up sharply** — NDX average daily volume is up roughly **4x since 2022**. Growth driven by interest in market-leading names and risk management across 1-day, 1-week, and longer-dated horizons.
- **Retail driving short-dated spread trading** — after Q2 2025 tariff-period weakness, NDX activity rebounded with significant retail participation and many short-dated spread trades.
- **Example hedge: NDX put butterfly** — host discusses a **300-point-wide NDX butterfly** around **29,000 / 28,700 / 28,400**, priced around **$1,300**, max profit around **$28k**. Use case: cheap convex downside hedge against many bullish single-name positions.
- **Core point: watch VXN/VIX ratio** — VXN divided by VIX had reached about **77% premium** by end of prior week. That means Nasdaq vol is far richer than S&P vol.
- **Why VXN premium is high** — risk and upside have concentrated in limited mega-cap / tech / semi names with outsized NDX weight. Similar dynamic appeared in 2017 during mega-cap tech outperformance.
- **Relative-vol trade idea** — Davitt looked at **30-day August 7 options**: sell an **8.5% OTM NDX put** around **26,800**, buy notionally adjusted SPX puts about **4.5% OTM**, same expiry. Structure still collects about **100 points** net premium.
- **Trade mechanics** — short expensive NDX downside vol; long cheaper SPX downside deltas/gamma. If market sells off broadly, SPX puts can balloon in delta and hedge more than initial delta suggests.
- **Risk: correlated selloff** — if everything sells off together, short NDX downside can hurt. Davitt explicitly says “big boy pants” required for NDX/SPX version.
- **Retail-sized alternative** — express similar relative idea with **XND and XSP**, but sizing caveat: need about **2.5x XND per XSP** to notionally align exposure.
- **Macro vol calm can hide sector stress** — software down while semis up, or vice versa, can keep broad index movement muted even as internal dispersion/risk is high.
- **Implied correlation matters** — NDX implied correlation is significantly higher than S&P implied correlation. That makes NDX useful as hedge for concentrated tech/semi exposure.
- **NDX may hedge single-name tech better than SPX** — S&P can be offset by energy/financials/defensives, while Nasdaq is more concentrated and directionally linked to current AI/semi risk.

## Trade / Hedge Checklist

- Existing book: many bullish tech/semi/single-name exposures.
- Problem: VIX too broad; S&P may not reflect true book risk.
- Better gauge: VXN and VXN/VIX ratio.
- Simple hedge: NDX put butterfly for cheap downside convexity.
- Relative trade: short richer NDX downside vol, long cheaper SPX downside protection.
- Must size notionally; NDX/SPX not 1:1.
- Main kill risk: correlated crash where NDX underperforms hard and short downside overwhelms hedge.
- Retail implementation: consider XND/XSP, but use correct multiplier/sizing.

## Edge / Risk Premia Frame

Structural edge claim is **relative volatility / concentration premium**: market is charging much more for Nasdaq downside because risk is concentrated in mega-cap tech/semi/AI. You get paid by selling that richer premium and funding broader-market downside protection. You are warehousing basis risk: NDX can fall more than SPX, especially in tech-led deleveraging.

## One-Line Summary

When AI/semi risk is concentrated in Nasdaq names, VXN matters more than VIX; NDX options offer cleaner hedges and relative-vol trades, but shorting rich NDX downside means owning real tech-crash basis risk.
