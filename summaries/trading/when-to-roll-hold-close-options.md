# When to Roll, Hold, or Close Options

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BTT7NLB4VKY

## TL;DR

Roll only when original thesis remains valid and added duration/premium creates a trade worth owning. Close when thesis is dead, profit is available, or capital can work better elsewhere. Hold only when remaining payoff justifies expiry/gamma risk. A roll changes exposure and defers realization; it does not erase loss.

## Decision framework

Before rolling, ask:

- Has underlying thesis changed?
- Is position near profitable range—“on the dance floor”?
- How much extrinsic value remains?
- Would you open rolled trade from scratch today?
- Is added credit worth capital, commissions, and extra tail duration?
- Should strikes or size change instead of mechanically preserving old position?

## Roll

Use when:

- Thesis remains intact.
- Position is ATM/near target and more time materially increases recovery probability.
- Existing short option has little extrinsic value and new cycle offers meaningful premium.
- New strike/expiry improves basis or delta without unacceptable tail risk.

Mechanics:

- Roll to later expiry for additional credit/time.
- Recenter strikes when spot moved materially.
- Reduce size/risk during roll when needed.
- Example: roll short AIG `49p` from October at about `$0.21` to November around `$1.04`, collecting roughly `$0.83–0.88`.

Trade-off: duration lowers immediate directional/gamma pressure but extends exposure and outside-tail risk.

## Hold

Use when:

- Little time remains and payoff still has favorable chance/value.
- Remaining premium is worth expiry risk.
- Losing debit spread has little residual value; holding as a small “lottery ticket” costs little more.

Do not hold tiny short premium while tying up large capital. Example: risking `$3k–4k` for final `$0.10` is poor asymmetry.

## Close

Use when:

- Thesis changed or underlying no longer matters.
- Debit spread is profitable/in the money.
- Most available profit already captured.
- Position is capital-inefficient or risk/reward deteriorated.
- Clean slate offers better opportunity than repeated salvage attempts.

## Important caveats

- “More time increases probability” does not mean lower total economic risk. It adds duration and more opportunities for adverse moves.
- Rolling for credit can hide accumulated loss; evaluate combined lifecycle P&L, not latest ticket.
- Advice comes from an older expiration-week clip. Modern Tastytrade framework usually manages still-open short premium around `21 DTE`, rather than waiting until expiration week.
- Short-vol edge comes from collecting volatility/tail-risk premium. Rolling only makes sense if remaining premium still compensates for that risk.

## One-line summary

Roll valid thesis, hold favorable residual payoff, close dead or capital-wasting exposure—and never pretend rolling converted loss into profit.
