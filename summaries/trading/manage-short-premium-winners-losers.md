# Manage Short-Premium Winners Early, Give Losers Time

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oWfBJJxE1MI

## TL;DR

For short-premium trades, Schultz reverses conventional momentum advice: take winners around 50% max profit because upside is capped, but do not automatically close a challenged option while it remains OTM/ATM with substantial time left. Theta decay and IV normalization may recover it. Small initial sizing preserves choices.

## Theory of edge

Short-premium seller earns volatility/convexity risk premium by warehousing gap, gamma, and tail risk. Time decay helps only if adverse delta and IV expansion do not overwhelm it.

## Winner management

- Credit received is maximum possible profit.
- As trade wins, remaining reward shrinks while open risk plus accrued profit remain exposed.
- General target: close around 50% max profit rather than chase final premium.

## Loser management

- Example: short OTM put; stock falls toward strike.
- Closing immediately realizes both adverse delta and higher-IV/vega losses.
- If put remains OTM or ATM at expiration, extrinsic value goes to zero; current mark-to-market loss may be temporary.
- If option moves deeply ITM and delta rises to 60–80, risk increasingly resembles long stock and requires active management.
- Possible responses: hold, roll, sell calls, reduce directional bias, or close.

## Sizing

- Schultz suggests roughly 3%–7% account value per undefined-risk position.
- Core principle matters more than exact range: position must be small enough that trader can choose rationally instead of being forced by margin or fear.

## Caveats

- “Give losers time” is not “hold indefinitely.” Tastytrade’s normal 21-DTE framework still treats 21 DTE as gamma/tail-risk cutoff for open winners and losers.
- Theta cannot rescue a genuine trend, gap, vol regime shift, or binary event.
- Undefined-risk sizing of 3%–7% per position can still become dangerous when correlations jump.
- Predefine delta, loss, DTE, event, and buying-power exits before entry.

## One-line summary

Harvest capped winners around 50%; give challenged short premium time only when position is small, expiry risk remains controlled, and invalidation has not fired.
