# Covered Calls: Think in Risk Equivalents

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=px10FrZh7_o

## TL;DR

A covered call is economically equivalent to a short put at the same strike and expiry. Both have capped profit, substantial downside, similar delta and theta. Calling covered-call premium “income” hides the real trade: bullish exposure plus short volatility/downside risk.

## Core identities

- `Long stock + long put = long call` (synthetic long call / protective put).
- `Long stock + short call = short put` (covered call).
- Example: stock `+100Δ` minus a `30Δ` short call leaves roughly `+70Δ`; equivalent short put carries roughly `+70Δ`.
- Same-strike put and call have similar extrinsic value, hence similar theta.

## Covered-call interpretation

- Better framing: short call reduces stock cost basis.
- Profit rises with stock until short-call strike, then caps.
- Premium cushions decline slightly; stock downside remains large.
- Edge, if any: selling volatility/upside convexity. Not free income.

## Why choose one implementation?

- Covered call: receives dividends, easier to roll call and retain stock; higher capital and two legs/commissions.
- Short put: often lower capital and one option leg; assignment possible.
- Same payoff can trade differently because of margin, dividends, liquidity, bid/ask, and commissions.
- Synthetic equivalents can help avoid wide spreads in deep-ITM options or flatten delta more cheaply.

## One-line summary

Covered call and short put are same risk trade in different packaging; choose by capital, liquidity, dividends, and execution—not “income” label.
