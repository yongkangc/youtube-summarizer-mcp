# Scaling the $100K+ Options Portfolio

## TL;DR

Scale breadth before concentration. Presenters roughly doubled deployed capital by adding five underlyings and increasing three existing positions while keeping SPY beta-weighted delta and estimated correlation almost unchanged.

## Exact changes

Before scaling:

- Capital used: about **$43k**.
- SPY beta-weighted delta: about **+62**.
- Correlation: about **0.35**.
- Existing basket: SPY, QQQ, IWM, FXI, euro, XOP and BITO.

Scaling:

- Added contracts to SPY, QQQ and IWM.
- Added TLT strangles.
- Added NVDA short puts.
- Added FSLR and EWZ short calls.
- Added JPM strangles.
- New capital: roughly **$40.4k**.
- Total capital used: about **$84.3k**.
- Beta-weighted delta moved only to approximately **+45**.
- Estimated correlation stayed near **0.35**.
- Additional premium: about **$7.9k**; total extrinsic around **$22k**.

They framed scaled book as appropriate for **$200k**, not $100k, and targeted retaining about **25% of extrinsic** or $5k–$6k. This is an expectation, not monthly yield.

## Main lessons

- Add independent underlyings and strategies before multiplying one position.
- Balance new bullish, bearish and neutral delta.
- Avoid using one or two large SPX positions merely because they consume buying power quickly.
- Neutral/index positions had worked better than directional opinions.
- Scaling should not materially increase beta-weighted direction or normal-period correlation.

## Main risk

Normal-period correlation and buying power understate crisis exposure. Short puts, calls and strangles can become one short-vol trade when equities gap, volatility rises and margin expands. At $84.3k deployed against a true $100k account, reserve would be dangerously thin.

## One-line summary

Scale a short-premium portfolio by adding diversified bets—not one giant bet—and verify stress risk stays controlled, not merely displayed delta.
