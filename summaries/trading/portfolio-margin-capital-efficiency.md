# My Trading Was This Inefficient Until This Study

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wQ3dPpy7nLI

## TL;DR

Tastytrade explains portfolio margin as a risk-based margin system that can reduce buying-power requirements versus standard margin, especially for larger, diversified, hedged, or uncorrelated portfolios. The warning: the trade’s economic risk is unchanged — lower BPR just means more leverage capacity, so capital usage rules must be tightened, not loosened blindly.

## Key points

- Portfolio margin is available for accounts with at least **$150k net liq**.
- Instead of static per-trade margin, PM uses live risk-based calculations at the **whole-portfolio** level.
- On average, PM lets traders hold similar positions with less capital than standard Reg-T margin.
- Tom compares PM capital requirements to futures/SPAN-style margining, but PM looks at the entire portfolio rather than each position alone.
- Tasty’s rule of thumb: if using futures options or portfolio margin, work with about **30% less capital allocation** than the standard-margin guideline.
- Their general max buying-power usage framework starts from “never more than **50%** of net liq” because options already contain leverage.
- They use three allocation regimes:
  - **Conservative**: low-capital-usage baseline.
  - **Moderate**: roughly **30% higher** than conservative; their usual default.
  - **Aggressive**: roughly **30% higher** than moderate; used in better-opportunity/high-vol regimes like April selloff.
- Current low-VIX environment: they say they are closer to conservative because “there’s just not much there.”
- In high-vol/high-opportunity periods, they may move toward aggressive.
- Earnings trades and high-IV single stocks can still carry higher BPR.
- Uncorrelated or hedged positions get more BPR relief than correlated positions.
- Same trade can have different BPR for different accounts because the calculation depends on existing positions.
- PM requirements are ultimately set by regulators/clearing bodies; brokers can raise requirements but Tasty says it passes through near-minimums.

## Trading read-through

Portfolio margin is not edge by itself. It is a capital-efficiency tool. The edge still has to come from short-vol/liquidity provision, relative value, hedging, or better deployment of scarce capital.

The danger is adverse selection through leverage: PM makes bad sizing easier. If BPR falls and you simply scale positions up until the account “feels” equally used, you may have increased tail risk without improving expected return. The right use is to free capital, improve hedging/diversification, and scale only when opportunity/IV justifies it.

## Practical checklist

- Treat PM as lower margin requirement, not lower risk.
- Keep net liq drawdown scenarios in dollars, not just BPR percentages.
- Use less allocation than Reg-T rules for the same risk target — Tasty says about **30% less**.
- Increase usage only when IV/opportunity is high, not when the broker simply allows it.
- Watch correlated positions: margin relief disappears when the whole book is one beta trade.
- Be extra careful with earnings/high-IV single-name trades; PM will not magically make them safe.

## One-line summary

Portfolio margin improves capital efficiency, but it turns sizing discipline into the whole game: same trade risk, lower BPR, easier to over-lever.
